Introduction to DSA

DSA 簡介

DSA, which stands for Data Sructures and Algorithms, is the class that teaches what to use in order to quickly store and handle information, depending on the situation. As it says on the tin, DSA is comprised of two larger topics:

  • Data Structures: Sometimes, arrays are simply not good enough to handle everything. They can be very slow, depending on what you do with them. There might better ways we can store information. Those are called data structures.
  • Algorithms: Algorithms are how the information is handled. The most basic algorithms are usually the slowest. To improve our speed, we will need better ways to handle information.

Things you need

上課預備

  1. Knowledge of limits. You'll need this for asymptotic analysis.
  2. Fluency in C. All your assignments will be in C.
    Specifically, you must know the following:
    • Everything up to pointers, i.e. loops, arrays, functions, and, of course, pointers
    • Data types larger than ints, namely unsigned long long.
    • Structs
    • Recursion (though mostly for the second semester)
    • Basic data structures, like linked lists and binary search trees
    You do not need to know these, but they are fairly useful:
    • Chars & strings
    • Bitwise operations
  3. A high tolerance for pain.
  1. 熟悉極限(limit)的概念
  2. 流利於C語言
  3. 耐於痛苦之心
